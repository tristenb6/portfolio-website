Sample Research Project

Academic • 2025 • Policy Analysis

Method: DiD Design: Quasi-Experimental Domain: Energy & Climate Data: Panel (2018–2024)

Abstract

This mock study estimates policy impacts on local outcomes using a transparent design, emphasizing reproducibility and clarity for applied audiences.

We combine administrative and survey data with a difference-in-differences estimator and robustness checks (event-study, placebo tests) to assess internal validity and mechanism plausibility.

Executive Summary

  • Policy associated with a 3–5% change in the main outcome.
  • Effects concentrated among disadvantaged tracts; heterogeneity aligned with exposure.
  • Results robust to alternative timing, specifications, and controls.

Methods & Design

Identification

  • Two-way fixed-effects DiD with staggered adoption.
  • Event-study to diagnose pre-trends and dynamic effects.
  • Placebo timing and leave-one-out sensitivity.

Data

  • Annual panel at county / tract level, 2018–2024.
  • Administrative outcomes merged to survey covariates.
  • Codebook and variable lineage documented for replication.

Key Findings

Effect Size

+4.2% (avg.)

Consistent across alt specs; strongest in high-exposure areas.

Robustness

Pre-trends flat

No significant anticipatory effects; timing falsifications pass.

Equity

Targeted gains

Larger impacts for disadvantaged tracts and frontline workers.
