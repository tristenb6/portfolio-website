Research Threads

Capstone on North Carolina’s energy transition, stakeholder mapping, and resilient storytelling—threads that shaped both the portfolio and this launch note.

Grounding the design in UNC blues and coastal greens felt right. It keeps me tethered to home while making space for the next chapter.

Launch Highlights

What started as a quick refresh turned into a two-day sprint of color palettes, layout sketches, and late-night copy edits. The goal was simple: build something that feels like UNC at twilight—warm, intentional, and a little bit electric.

Why launch now?

Wrapping up graduate school was the perfect forcing function. A new site marks the shift from student to practitioner.

What’s inside?

Academic projects with policy impact, photography that keeps me curious, and a hub for future collaborations.

How it feels

Playful but purposeful. Every hover, highlight, and badge nods to the people-first work I care about.

UNC Reflections

UNC was a laboratory for building partnerships around energy and climate resilience. The capstone that anchors the academic section of this site—mapping narratives around North Carolina’s energy transition—reminded me that policy stories land best when stakeholders can see themselves in the data.

“Good analysis is useful. Great analysis is applicable. UNC gave me room to practice both.”

Stakeholder interviews

Listening sessions with advocates, utilities, and communities revealed shared hopes for equitable transition—even when tactics differed.

Energy transition workshop

Facilitated a workshop translating research into decision-ready insights for state partners—a reminder that visuals matter as much as numbers.

Final campus dusk

Walked past the Old Well one last time, thinking about how Carolina’s sense of community shows up in this site’s tone and typography.

Build Notes

The portfolio leans on semantic HTML and a custom CSS system with layered gradients, pressed buttons, and texture that nods to analog print. A dash of JavaScript handles the niceties—lazy-loaded images, reading progress, and micro-interactions that keep the experience light.

Interaction Touches
  • 3D card hover states borrowed from the photography grid now anchor academic projects too.
  • Quick-nav and reading bar were tuned for this article—small UX lifts that encourage exploring.
  • Fonts: Syne for punchy headers, Inter for body, and a new Playfair Display accent for blog storytelling.
